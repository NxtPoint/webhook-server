Sub-processors

Last updated: 2026-06-18

To deliver Ten-Fifty5 we rely on a small set of vetted third-party providers ("sub-processors") that process personal data on our behalf. This page lists each one, what it does, the categories of data involved, and where the data is processed together with the transfer safeguards in place. It complements our Privacy Policy.

We do not sell your data. We share it with these providers strictly to run the service, under contracts that require them to protect it and process it only on our instructions. For each US provider we maintain a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) plus the 2021 Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — relying on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF) where the provider is validly certified, with the SCCs as the fallback — and a UK Addendum for UK data flows.

PayPal acts as an independent controller for the payment data it handles, not as our processor — it is disclosed here as a data recipient for transparency. We store no card details ourselves.

Current sub-processors

Provider Purpose Data categories Region / transfer basis
Amazon Web Services (AWS) Hosting, storage (S3), GPU processing, transactional email (SES) Account data, match video, derived analytics, biometric (pose) data, service email US (us-east-1) & EU (eu-north-1) · DPA + SCCs (DPF where certified); UK Addendum
SportAI Match video analysis Uploaded match video and derived match statistics Processed under DPA + SCCs where applicable
Clerk Authentication (login, account, session) Name, email, login / session credentials US · DPA + SCCs (DPF where certified); UK Addendum

Klaviyo Marketing email (lifecycle / opt-in only) Name, email, marketing engagement — never minors' data or biometrics US · DPA + SCCs (DPF where certified); UK Addendum
PayPal Payments Payment / transaction data (we store no card data) US · Independent controller for payment data; SCCs where applicable
Amplitude Product analytics Behavioural usage events (logins, uploads, report views) US · DPA + SCCs (DPF where certified); UK Addendum

HubSpot has been removed and is no longer used — we are our own CRM. It is dormant and receives no data.

Changes to this list

We may add or change sub-processors as the service evolves. When we do, we will update this page and the "last updated" date above. Where required, we will give advance notice of material changes. Questions about our sub-processors or a specific request: info@ten-fifty5.com.
