## 5. How to request access to a record

1. Use the **prescribed PAIA request form (Form 02)** issued by the Regulator (available at
   https://inforegulator.org.za).
2. Send the completed form to the Information Officer at **info@ten-fifty5.com**.
3. Provide: sufficient detail to identify the record; the form of access required; your contact details; and,
   if requesting on behalf of someone, proof of authority.
4. If the request is for **your own personal information** under POPIA, no fee applies to the request itself
   (see §7); for other records, the prescribed PAIA fees may apply.
5. The Information Officer will respond **within 30 days** (extendable by a further 30 days where permitted,
   with reasons given to you).

## 6. Grounds for refusal of access (PAIA Chapter 4)
Access may be refused on the mandatory/discretionary grounds in PAIA, including: protection of the **privacy of
a third party**; **commercial information** of the Company or a third party; **confidential information**;
records protected by **legal professional privilege**; and where disclosure is prohibited by other law. Where
access is refused, you will be given reasons and informed of your remedies.

## 7. Prescribed fees
PAIA prescribes (a) a **request fee** (not applicable to a request for your own personal information), and
(b) an **access fee** based on reproduction/preparation costs. The current prescribed amounts are set by
Regulation under PAIA; the Information Officer will provide a fee estimate before processing where a fee
applies, and may require a deposit for lengthy preparation.

## 8. Remedies — internal and external
- **Complaint to the Information Regulator:** a requester may submit a complaint to the Regulator about a
  refusal or the handling of a request (PAIAComplaints@inforegulator.org.za).
- **Court application:** a requester may apply to court for appropriate relief after exhausting available
  remedies.

## 9. POPIA additions (processing of personal information)

In terms of POPIA, this manual also records the following about the Company's processing of personal
information. Full detail is in the Company's [Privacy Policy](privacy-and-consent.md).

### 9.1 Purpose of processing
To deliver tennis match and technique analysis to customers; to operate, secure and improve the service; to
communicate with customers; to send marketing to those who opt in; and to meet legal/tax obligations.

### 9.2 Categories of data subjects and their personal information
| Data subject | Personal information |
|---|---|
| Adult account holders | Name, email, phone, country, account/usage data, marketing consent |
| Junior players (managed by an adult) | Name, skill level, optional DOB/club; match clips and derived stats — processed only with parental/guardian consent |
| Coaches | Name, email, permission/links granted by players |
| Incidental third parties (people in uploaded footage) | Image, incidental to the customer's analysis |

### 9.3 Recipients / operators (sub-processors)
AWS (hosting, compute, email), Anthropic (AI features), Clerk (authentication), PayPal (payments — likely an
independent responsible party), Klaviyo (opt-in marketing only — never minors'/biometric data), SportAI (match
analysis), Amplitude (product analytics). Each is bound by a written operator/processing agreement.

### 9.4 Cross-border (trans-border) flows — POPIA s72
Personal information may be processed outside South Africa (EU `eu-north-1`, US `us-east-1`). Transfers rely on
**binding agreements providing protection substantially similar to POPIA** (data-processing agreements
incorporating Standard Contractual Clauses; the EU-US Data Privacy Framework where a recipient is validly
certified), and/or the data subject's consent or contractual necessity, as required by POPIA s72.

### 9.5 Special personal information and children's information
The Company processes **children's** personal information **only with the consent of a competent person**
(parent/guardian) per POPIA s35. **Biometric (pose) data is processed transiently and never stored**, with
explicit (parental) consent before technique analysis, and is never used to identify a person.

### 9.6 Security safeguards (POPIA s19)
Access controls, encryption in transit, data minimisation (deletion of originals and pose data), and use of
reputable processors under written agreements. The Company maintains a retention schedule and automated
deletion of records no longer required.

### 9.7 Data-subject rights
Data subjects may request access to, and correction or deletion of, their personal information, object to
processing, and withdraw consent, by emailing **info@ten-fifty5.com**. Requests are handled within the
timeframes required by PAIA/POPIA.
